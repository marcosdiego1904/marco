Bon Appetit! Liverpool-City gave us a soccer feast

Once again the two titans showed us that they are the top of the elite, and why the premier league is the best league in the world.

Marcos Barzola

Last Sunday we lived one of the most exciting matches of the year, in my opinion the most similar to a Madrid-Barca in its prime. This match not only stood out for the soccer quality of both teams but also for what was at stake. Liverpool came with a point over City or tied with Arsenal, a victory for either of them could have been a blow on the table and this context seemed to be that gave more hunger to the two teams which seemed to have been playing a final.

Getting a little more into the actions of the match, city started very strong with several chances to score, however liverpool was able to recover and imposed its well known intensity. Despite Liverpool taking the lead in the first half, City were able to surprise through a corner which they converted into a goal. However, Liverpool did not give up and even seemed to have upped the intensity, this pattern of play continued until the second half, where in a pressure by Darwin Nu√±ez, the Uruguayan received a penalty foul which McAllister converted into a goal.

This fact meant a drop in spirits for city, which surprisingly could not recover throughout the second half. Liverpool never lowered their intensity which gave them clear chances that they could not convert into goals. The final summary of the match is that Liverpool ended up wanting more and City asking for the time. the ones who undoubtedly won were the soccer lovers like you and me, who ended up satisfied with the soccer banquet that these two great clubs offered us.
