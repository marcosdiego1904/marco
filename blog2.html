Barcelona, Pass the Exam.

Barcelona did their homework by eliminating Napoli and secured their place in the quarterfinals

Marcos Barzola

After 4 years of wandering around the Europa league, Barcelona was once again faced with the opportunity to be among the 8 best teams in the world. Facing them was Napoli, an Italian team with a managerial and sporting crisis, having a surprisingly unrecognizable image this season after having been league champion last season.

If there is one thing that cannot be denied about Barcelona is that it is a soccer great, which means that it has a great history and above all a great fan base, and in this test against Napoli we could say that these were Barcelona's most lethal weapons, since in terms of soccer Xavi's team is still in need of improvement.

Prior to the match, outside the stadium an atmosphere was created by the Barca fans that had not been seen in years and support was given until the end of the match, the fans became the 12th player encouraging a Barca that for parts of the match their legs trembled. In the first 25 minutes Barca was very solid, but as usual the team suffered many ups and downs which led to Napoli's goal, this made the legs tremble even the most cules fans who were reminded of the tragic nights of previous years, in the end Barca knew how to recover from the adversity and sealed the qualification with a great play by Lewandoski.

Barca is now part of the prestigious group of the top 8 and will be waiting to find out who will be their opponent this Friday.
